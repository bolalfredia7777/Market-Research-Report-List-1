<p><p>Linear Low-density Polyethylene (LLDPE) is a type of polyethylene characterized by its flexibility, strength, and resistance to impact. It is produced through the copolymerization of ethylene with α-olefins, resulting in a polymer with lower density and improved mechanical properties compared to low-density polyethylene (LDPE). LLDPE is widely utilized in applications such as packaging films, containers, and agricultural films, owing to its excellent barrier properties and durability.</p><p>The LLDPE market is experiencing significant growth, primarily driven by increasing demand for flexible packaging, particularly in the food and beverage sectors.
