<p><p>Sonic nozzles are utilized in various applications based on their operational systems, primarily categorized into pressurized and vacuum systems. Pressurized systems operate by allowing gas to expand through the nozzle, achieving sonic velocity, ideal for processes requiring precise flow rates. In contrast, vacuum systems utilize low-pressure environments to facilitate gas flow, often suited for applications where controlling vapor extraction is crucial. Both types ensure efficient flow measurement and regulation in diverse industrial processes, including aerospace and chemical manufacturing.</p></p>
