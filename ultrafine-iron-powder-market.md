<p><p>Ultrafine iron powder is a finely divided form of iron used in various industries, including electronics, automotive, and metallurgy. Carbonyl iron powder is produced by thermal decomposition of iron pentacarbonyl, resulting in high purity and spherical particles ideal for magnetic applications and coatings. Electrolytic iron powder is made through electrolysis, offering high purity and irregular shapes, suitable for powder metallurgy and chemical processes. Both types cater to specialized applications, with carbonyl powder excelling in precision and electrolytic powder in structural uses.</p></p>
