<p><p>Thin film photovoltaic (PV) cells encompass various technologies, primarily Gallium Arsenide (GaAs), Copper Indium Selenium (CIS), and Cadmium Telluride (CdTe). GaAs cells are known for their high efficiency and performance in space applications but are costly. CIS cells offer a balance between efficiency and cost, suitable for both residential and commercial use. CdTe cells are more affordable and widely used in utility-scale solar farms, known for their low environmental impact during production. Each type caters to specific market needs.</p></p>
