<p><p>Laboratory Grade Water is categorized into three grades based on purity and application. </p><p>Grade 1 water, also known as ultrapure water, has the highest purity and is suitable for sensitive analytical procedures, such as HPLC or mass spectrometry. </p><p>Grade 2 water is suitable for most laboratory applications, including buffer preparation and microbiological tests, while Grade 3 water is used for general laboratory work, such as rinsing glassware and preparing non-critical solutions. Each grade serves specific requirements to ensure reproducibility and accuracy in experiments.</p></p>
