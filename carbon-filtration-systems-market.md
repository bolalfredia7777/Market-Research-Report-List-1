<p><p>Carbon filtration systems utilize different types of activated carbon to purify air and water. Granular Activated Carbon (GAC) consists of small granules that effectively adsorb pollutants, making it ideal for larger-scale applications like water treatment. Block Carbon, on the other hand, features denser, more compact forms, providing a longer lifespan and higher filtration efficiency in smaller devices, such as residential air purifiers. Both types play crucial roles in enhancing environmental quality by removing contaminants and improving safety.</p></p>
