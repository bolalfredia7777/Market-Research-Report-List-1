<p><p>The aerospace carbon fiber market is primarily divided into regular-tow and large-tow carbon fibers. Regular-tow carbon fiber consists of thinner filaments, offering high strength and stiffness, making it ideal for applications requiring precision and intricate designs. Conversely, large-tow carbon fiber features thicker filaments, providing a cost-efficient option with greater productivity and reduced manufacturing times, suitable for larger, less complex structures. Each type serves distinct roles in aerospace applications, balancing performance, weight, and cost considerations.</p></p>
